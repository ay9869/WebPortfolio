Tomato Leaf Disease Detection:

Computer vision has emerged as a vital tool in agriculture for monitoring plant health and diagnosing diseases. This research proposes a deep learning-based approach using the VGG19 architecture to detect tomato leaf diseases. The method is capable of automatically identifying nine major tomato leaf diseases, including bacterial spot, early blight, late blight, and spider mite damage, enabling efficient and accurate plant health assessment.


Key features of the dashboard include:

  • Real-Time Results: Immediate disease detection and analysis with up-to-date visual feedback.
  • Dynamic Filters: Tailor views by disease type, severity, or time period for precise analysis.
  • Predictive Insights: Leverage data trends to forecast disease spread and recommend preventive measures.

With a user-friendly interface and predictive capabilities, this innovation brings precision and efficiency to plant disease management, driving smarter agricultural practices.
